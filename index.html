Resources for your GIS lab session at TMU

Welcome to the Department of Geography and Environmental Studies at Toronto Metropolitan University, formerly known as Ryerson University! We offer GIS lab sessions for high school students to showcase our BA in Geographic Analysis and the wide range of professional applications of geographic information systems. The GIS lab is located on the 3rd floor of the Podium building, room POD-348 (enter from Victoria Street, JOR building, see campus map).

Let's explore the following web apps:

  • Prizm neighbourhood profile lookup (geodemographics by Environics Analytics used in marketing and many other client-focused applications)
  • Trade area modeling tutorial (business intelligence by alum Jack Forsyth for location decisions and retail sales prediction)
  • Public Safety Data Portal | Maps (crime analytics by Toronto Police Service for prevention and community outreach)
  • Wellbeing Toronto (socio-economic monitoring by City of Toronto, Social Policy Analysis & Research, for planning of social services and infrastructure investments)
  • COVID-19 dashboard and charts (public health applications by Johns Hopkins University and "Our World in Data", for infectious disease monitoring and pandemic response)

Visit the Geographic Analysis program homepage for more information including the student experience, student internship reports, alumni news and interviews, and program curriculum. For details on how to apply, visit the undergraduate admissions pages.
