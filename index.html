Resources for your GIS lab session at TMU

Welcome to the Department of Geography and Environmental Studies at Toronto Metropolitan University, formerly known as Ryerson University! We offer GIS lab sessions for high school students and applicants to showcase our BA (Hons) in Geographic Analysis and the wide range of professional applications of geographic information systems. The GIS lab is located on the 3rd floor of the Podium building, room POD-348 (enter at 380 Victoria Street, JOR building, see campus map).

Let's explore some of the following web apps to see Geographic Analysis in action:

  • The True Size Of ...: Global map application that illustrates fundamental challenges with accurate map-making.
  • Prizm neighbourhood profile lookup: Geodemographics data by Environics Analytics used in marketing and many other client-focused applications.
  • Trade area modeling tutorial by TMU Geography alum Jack Forsyth for location decisions and retail sales prediction.
  • Public Safety Data Portal | Maps: Crime analytics and community resource mapps by Toronto Police Service.
  • Wellbeing Toronto: Planning of social services and infrastructure investments by City of Toronto, Social Policy Analysis & Research unit.
  • Ontario Watershed Information Tool: "Learn how to make watershed maps and calculate watershed characteristics."
  • COVID-19 dashboard and charts: Public health applications by Johns Hopkins University and "Our World in Data" for infectious disease monitoring and pandemic response.
